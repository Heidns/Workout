RPE — Rate of Perceived Exertion

A 1–10 scale for how hard you're working. No equipment needed — it's purely how you feel. Use it to pick the right weight for each set.

RPE 8 = your target. After finishing your 10–12 reps, you should feel like you could do about 2 more good, clean reps before hitting failure. If you could do 5 more, go heavier. If you couldn't finish, go lighter.
8 / 10 — 2 reps left in the tank

Progressive Overload — how to keep growing

Method 1 — Increase Weight
1. Keep the same rep target every week.
2. Once you can hit all reps cleanly, add weight next session.
3. All sets taken to failure.
Example — Incline DB Press
Week 1: 20 kg × 13, 12, 10 reps
Week 2: 22.5 kg × 13, 12, 10 reps ↑
Method 2 — Double Progression
Recommended
1. Pick a weight you can do 8 clean reps with.
2. Add 1 rep each week until you hit 12.
3. Once you hit 12, increase weight and drop back to 8.
4. Repeat — both reps and weight climb over time.
Example — 8–12 Rep Range
Week 1: 22.5 kg × 8
Week 2: 22.5 kg × 9
Week 3–5: … × 10, 11, 12
Week 6: 25 kg × 8 ↑
